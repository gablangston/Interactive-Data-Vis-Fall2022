An Overview on Speed Cameras in NYC

Speed cameras have been a hot topic in New York City. Various advocacy groups from Transportation Alternatives to Families for Safe Streets have been asking for reforms to ensure public safety. This past summer, Governor Hochul signed a bill which requires speed cameras to be on 24/7. Currently, there are approximately 750 cameras citywide, and drivers who exceed 10 mph over the speed limit are charged a $50 fine. As part of the legislation, speed cameras must be within a 1,320-foot radius of a school entrance or exit.
